    Thinking about choosing polished concrete floors? Talk to your Houston concrete supply! Polished concrete is becoming a popular flooring choice for many different types of buildings, including homes, offices, warehouses, and retail spaces. We’ll discuss why and how your concrete contractor can tailor your concrete mix to help you create an aesthetically pleasing base for polished concrete.

    Benefits of Polished Concrete Floors

    It’s no wonder why many people are asking our Houston concrete supply about polished concrete floors. This flooring option is less expensive per square foot than other flooring choices and is incredibly low maintenance. What’s more, it’s durable and long-lasting, even in high traffic applications. So, they’re cheaper to install and help save on maintenance costs.

    Polished concrete floors are also sure to impress with their high gloss finish. They’re attractive and also help reflect light to provide brighter interiors and increased visibility. Even though they are shiny, they’re slip-resistant. In fact, walking on polished marble or waxed linoleum offers a higher risk of slipping and falling.

    You can often find polished concrete floors in warehouses and other industrial facilities because of these great benefits. However, these types of floors are becoming more and more popular for other types of buildings.

    If you’re interested in polished concrete floors for your building, you may even be able to polish older concrete. However, if it’s damaged or extremely porous, you might need new concrete from a concrete contractor. Also, new concrete may offer more customization options to help you achieve a unique look for your flooring.

    How Your Houston Concrete Supply can Help You Customize Concrete for Polished Concrete Floors

    The great thing about Houston ready mix concrete is that it’s entirely tailor-made for your project. This gives you a huge range of choices to help you alter the finished look of your polished concrete floors.

    Now, you might know that polishing concrete offers different shine levels, but don’t forget to consider the concrete itself. For instance, in many cases your concrete contractor can add dyes to the mix itself to help you achieve a certain look. Also, different aggregates can affect the look of your finished concrete, too. For example, you might choose coarse aggregates for lots of texture, or fine aggregates for a really sleek and smooth look. Your concrete contractors can also help make sure the concrete mix is specifically designed for your application, such as a high traffic commercial floor or a residential floor for your home.
