  • Best Types of Sealers to Use on Houston Ready Mix Concrete

    Posted on March 21, 2023 by Phoebee Johnson

    Ready mix concrete is a great option for many projects. Adding sealers after curing can improve durability and longevity.

    Houston ready mix concrete is a very versatile material, and it can be used in a variety of ways. It’s highly durable and long-lasting. You can also improve the durability and lifespan of your concrete by sealing it with special sealers. This helps reduce how porous the concrete is and stop things like stains, weathering, and other types of damage. There are many different types of concrete sealers available on the market, so it’s important to choose the right one for your needs. In this blog post, we’ll discuss the best types of sealers to use on concrete surfaces.

    Why Does Houston Ready Mix Concrete Need a Sealant?

    To ensure your ready mix concrete lasts for years to come, it’s a good idea to seal the surface with a concrete sealer. Sealing works like a moisture barrier, preventing water and other liquids from seeping in and corroding the surface. It also gives concrete an added layer of protection against things like oil, leaf debris, and other staining materials. By sealing concrete you not only improve its durability but add additional aesthetic value as well! That’s why our Houston concrete supply often recommends applying a sealer after the concrete fully cures.

    Types of Concrete Sealers

    When it comes to protecting concrete from weathering, there are plenty of concrete sealers to choose from, including:

    • Acrylic
    • Epoxy
    • Urethane

    If your outdoor concrete is exposed to rain and harsh sunlight, then you’ll want a transparent acrylic sealer. These concrete sealers will provide the maximum protection against moisture and ultraviolet exposure. Acrylic sealers add a slightly glossy finish to your Houston ready mix concrete, though you can find matte options as well.

    For interior concrete surfaces, an epoxy sealer can be applied. Not only does it provide an attractive glossy finish, but it’s also extremely durable, making it great for high traffic areas like hallways and lobbies.

    For extra protection against everyday wear and tear think about using a urethane sealer. This will increase the lifespan of your surface considerably. All in all, with so many options available each offering their own unique features and benefits – it’s easy to find the perfect sealer for your concrete project!

    Applying Sealers to Houston Ready Mix Concrete

    Applying concrete sealers is a tried-and-true method of protecting and extending the life of your concrete surfaces. But in order to ensure that you properly protect your surface, it’s important to take certain steps when applying the sealer:

    1. Make sure the ready mix concrete is fully cured
    2. Clean the surface so it’s free of dirt and debris
    3. Test a small sample area to make sure you like the final look
    4. Paint or spray the sealer on evenly across the whole surface
    5. Let the sealer dry overnight

    Following these tips should go a long way in making sure you apply your concrete sealers perfectly!

    Don’t Make these Common Mistakes

    Concrete sealers can be a great way to beautify and protect the surface of concrete, but they are not without their drawbacks. Without proper maintenance, concrete sealers can actually cause more harm than good by wearing out quickly or fading over time; not to mention the mess they can create if applied inaccurately! Make sure you know how to avoid these common mistakes.

    Over-sealing is one common mistake that people make when using concrete sealers. Applying too many layers of detailing won’t necessarily add more protection. In fact, it may trap moisture underneath and create a myriad of issues. Additionally, uneven coverage and missed spots are among other crucial mistakes to avoid so ensure you give your sealed concrete surface plenty of time to dry before walking on it and use a non-slip finish for extra safety measures.
