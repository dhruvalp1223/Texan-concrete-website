  • The Different Formulations of Concrete and Their Uses

    Posted on July 20, 2017 by Bizadmin

    Choosing the right type of concrete for your commercial or residential project can ensure the greatest durability and longevity for these applications. Working with a trusted Houston concrete supply company can allow you to enjoy the most accurate guidance and support throughout your construction process. Here are some general guidelines on the different types of concrete and their most practical uses.

    Standard Concrete

    The formulations used to create standard concrete can vary within a relatively narrow band of tolerances. Depending on the use to which this material will be put, the ratio between cement, aggregate and water can differ significantly. Standard mix is prepared at a Houston concrete supply plant for delivery instead of at the construction site.

    High-Strength and High-Performance Concrete

    For building construction, high-strength and high-performance concrete formulations offer much greater compressive strength. These mixes are created by reducing the water-cement ratio and adding chemical compounds to increase the durability and the density of the concrete.

    Pervious Concrete

    Your Houston concrete contractor may recommend pervious concrete for parking lots and access roads to your property. This advanced concrete formulation is designed to allow water to pass through tiny holes in the surface and through the pavement. This can reduce the amount of runoff, skidding on wet roads and can have significant benefits for the surrounding environment.

    Lightweight Concrete

    The lower weight and increased insulating power of lightweight concrete makes it an ideal choice for building interior walls. It is also suited for reducing the weight applied to foundations, especially for taller buildings. These building materials can also provide added help in reducing the spread of fires thanks to its flame-resistant characteristics.

    Self-Consolidating Concrete

    Also referred to as self-leveling concrete, these formulations are designed to create a smooth and level surface without the need for vibration or other methods for evening out the concrete. These mixtures depend on chemical additives that increase the plasticity of the concrete and allow it to flow together seamlessly for a near-perfect finish every time.

    Fiber-Reinforced Concrete

    Combining the strength of steel and synthetic fibers with the practical ease of use of pourable concrete, fiber-reinforced formulations are ideal for use in driveways, sidewalks, decks and for commercial and residential construction projects. These advanced materials are becoming more popular among Houston builders and project managers.
