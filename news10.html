    What is Concrete Slump? Why is it Important to Know When You Talk to Your Houston Concrete Supply?

    he concept of concrete slump is used in the construction industry to indicate how much a batch of concrete mix will settle when fresh. It’s an important metric for assessing the workability of the mixture, as too much or too little settlement can affect the strength and stability of concrete structures. The amount of water that is present in the mix determines the slump. The more water that is present, the higher the slump. Slump tells you when a pour is ready and how easy it will be to work with. Too much or too little water can create problems. Usually, you will set concrete slump with your concrete supplier in Houston to ensure correct formulation. Most projects require a concrete slump range between zero and four inches. Ultimately, understanding how concrete slump works gives you peace of mind that their projects will be completed successfully and safely. You can work with your Houston concrete supply to create concrete with the right slump for your project.

    Define Concrete Slump When Ordering Ready Mix Concrete

    Understanding the slump of your concrete mix can make or break your project. When it comes to concrete, the workability and strength depend on the slump. Having too much or too little slump affects how easy it is to put the concrete in place and how much stress that same concrete will be able to take later. Knowing just how much combined moisture and cement paste are needed is essential if you want to make sure that your finished project lasts as long as it should. Paying attention to the slump before beginning your concreting job can save you time, money and hassle down the road. If you’re not sure, work with the experts at our Houston concrete supply for help.

    When it comes to ready mix concrete, it’s essential to know the slump of the concrete to ensure you receive the right formula. Concrete experts will ensure it’s correct using a slump test. A concrete slump test checks the consistency of ready mix concrete just before use. This way, they can assess the workability and strength before you use it for your project.

    How to Test Concrete Slump

    Testing the slump of concrete involves determining if a mix is too dry, too wet, or just right. With a little practice, testing concrete slump should not be overly difficult. As we mentioned, your Houston concrete supply should do this before delivery, but you can also test before pouring just to make sure.

    To start out, scoop the concrete into a plastic cup with a hole in the bottom. Then, flip the cup upside down on a flat surface and lift so the concrete comes out of the cup. From there, you can measure the slump. If the concrete is the same height as the cup, then there’s zero inch slump and has very little water. If it’s an inch shorter, then it has one inch slump, and so on.
