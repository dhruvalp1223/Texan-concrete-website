  • Houston Concrete Supply: Why Limestone Aggregates?

    Posted on February 19, 2021 by Phoebee Johnson

    Limestone aggregates from your Houston concrete supply add bulk and strength to cement.

    When you need concrete, your Houston concrete supply creates a formula based on your needs and budget. One important part of creating a custom concrete mix for your project is choosing the right aggregates for your concrete. Limestone is a common choice for concrete aggregates and offers many benefits. Talk to your concrete specialists about limestone aggregates for your concrete formula.

    Ask your Houston Concrete Supply About Limestone Aggregates

    A common type of aggregate for concrete is limestone. Limestone is a type of sedimentary rock found throughout the world and is made primarily of calcium carbonate. When it comes to Houston cement mixing, limestone aggregates are quite affordable, with pricing similar to that of gravel. However, there are some distinct benefits of using limestone aggregates over gravel. First, limestone aggregates are generally bulkier. They are also generally more jagged compared to gravel. This allows the cement to bond to limestone aggregates better than the smooth surfaces of gravel. This can lead to stronger, more durable concrete. In fact, concrete with limestone aggregates from your Houston concrete supply can be up to ten percent stronger compared to concrete with gravel.

    Also, limestone is less prone to expanding in the heat and contracting in the cold. This is why limestone aggregates are a common choice for many concrete formulas. One of the most common causes of concrete cracks is due to expansion and contraction in different temperatures. Limestone aggregates can help reduce this, which means more durable, longer-lasting concrete.

    Limestone comes in varying shades of gray. Your Houston concrete supply will work with you to find the colors and types of limestone aggregates to suit your aesthetic. Limestone aggregates generally offer a more uniform appearance and color to your concrete compared to gravel and river stone aggregates.

    Why Are Aggregates Important?

    You may be wondering why you need aggregates at all for your concrete. Your Houston concrete supplier uses aggregates to make your concrete strong, durable, and long-lasting. Generally, aggregates make up between 60-80% of your concrete. Many people wrongly assume that aggregates are merely filler for your concrete formulation. However, the aggregate you choose actually affects the properties of your concrete. Aggregates can affect the cement laying process as well as how strong your concrete is after it hardens.
