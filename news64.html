  • How to Power Wash Concrete and Remove Stains Safely

    Posted on September 21, 2018 by Bizadmin

    Once your Houston concrete contractor has finished your project, the responsibility for maintaining your concrete is yours. Making sure you know how to clean your concrete properly can help your driveway, sidewalk or patio look its best for many years to come. Here are some of the most helpful tips for keeping your concrete clean and attractive for added curb appeal.

    Use a Pressure Washer for Faster Cleaning

    A pressure washer, as its name suggests, is designed to spray water at high pressure against surfaces for cleaning. These power washers are often used to clean the sides of buildings and can provide the power needed to remove oil, dirt and other stains from driveways, patios and sidewalks.

    • Choose a hot-water pressure washer to make short work of oil spills and ground-in stains on your concrete driveway. Hot water cleans much more quickly than cold water and is better at dissolving oil and dirt.
    • Your power washer should have a pressure rating of at least 3000 psi and a flow rate between four and six gallons per minute to achieve the best results for your cleaning project.
    • For even faster cleaning and moving, you can opt for a wheel-mounted power washer that can be pushed over the surface of your driveway or patio. This can allow you to complete your concrete washing tasks in much less time and with about the same amount of effort as push-mowing your yard.

    Using a pressure washer to clean your Houston ready mix concrete can speed up the job and help you to achieve the most beautiful results for patios, driveways and other concrete around your home or business.

    Apply Concrete Detergent

    Specialized detergent formulations can offer added help in removing concrete stains, oil and grease. Your Houston concrete supply company can usually provide you with recommendations on the right concrete detergent for your needs. Most concrete cleaning professionals recommend leaving the detergent to soak for about 10 minutes before rinsing it with a pressure washer or hose. This can produce the best possible results for your concrete restoration project.
