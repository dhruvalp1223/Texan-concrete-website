    Supplies for Painting Houston Ready Mix Concrete Surfaces

    Painting concrete surfaces can be a great way to add a pop of color or update the look of your outdoor space. To get started, you will need a few key items.

    First and foremost, you’ll need paint that is specifically designed for use on concrete surfaces. This type of paint is formulated to withstand the harsh outdoor elements and provide a long-lasting finish. These can include:

    • Concrete porch and patio paints: low-luster acrylic latex paints that conceal imperfections and resist scuffing and fading.
    • Epoxy-based paints: highly durable and stain resistant, ideal for garage floors or patios.
    • Masonry paints: specifically designed for brick and stone, but can also be an effective coating for concrete.

    Additionally, you’ll want to ensure you have the right type of paint roller. The best paint roller for concrete should have a ½ to ¾ inch nap, which helps apply paint evenly to a rough surface. A thicker nap holds more paint, which helps to fill cracks and crevices in the concrete surface. The standard width of a roller is 9 inches, but you can also get an industrial size roller that is 14 to 18 inches wide to help you get your painting job done faster.

    With the right tools and materials, you’ll be on your way to transforming your concrete surfaces in no time.

    Preparing a Concrete Surface for Painting

    If you’re looking to give your concrete surface a fresh new look with a coat of paint, it’s important to properly prepare the surface beforehand. As much as possible, you should remove the remnants of previous coatings or sealants. Otherwise, the paint may not bond to the surface properly.

    It’s very important to thoroughly remove any dirt, debris, or oil. This can be done by pressure washing or scrubbing with a cleaning solution. After washing, make sure the surface is fully dry. An easy way to check this is to tape a piece of clear plastic to the surface and leave it for 24 hours. If condensation forms under the plastic, the surface is still too damp for painting.

    It’s also important to repair any significant cracks or damage before painting to ensure a smooth and even surface. Taking the time to properly prepare your concrete surface before painting will help ensure a long-lasting and beautiful finish.

    Applying Primer and Paint to Concrete

    Before you start slathering on the color, it’s important to prep the surface with primer. Applying primer to a concrete surface is essential for ensuring that the paint adheres properly and lasts as long as possible. Not only does primer help the paint stick to the surface, but it also smooths out any rough patches or imperfections, resulting in a flawless finish. Two coats of primer are often recommended for best results, with at least two hours of drying time per coat.

    Once the primer is dry, it’s time to start applying the paint. Start with the edges and then fill in the center portion. Don’t rush! Whether you’re applying a single color or a complex design, taking the time to apply the paint correctly will make all that time you spent preparing worth it.
