  • Houston Ready Mix Concrete vs. Site-Mixed Concrete

    Posted on February 21, 2022 by Phoebee Johnson

    We mix Houston ready mix concrete in cement mixing trucks and deliver it to your construction site.

    If you have a project and you need concrete, you might wonder what your options are. Contractors typically choose one of two options: Houston ready mix concrete and site-mixed concrete. These are two different methods for mixing concrete. Let’s go over the differences between the two and the pros and cons of each.

    What is Houston Ready Mix Concrete?

    Ready mix concrete is available from your Houston concrete supplier. Concrete experts formulate this type of concrete specifically for your project. The concrete is precisely batched at our facility and mixed in a cement mixer truck while en route to your project site.

    Ready mixed concrete offers some unique benefits for your concrete project. The first is that our experts completely customize the formula based on your project. This concrete option also offers more exact formulation and mixing to maximize strength and performance. Precise mixing at our state-of-the-art facility also means you get exact quantities for less waste.

    Another great reason to choose Houston ready mix concrete for your project is that there are much fewer logistics to sort out. All you do is tell us about your project and the date and time you need it delivered. We take care of everything from there. There’s no need to worry about when you need to mix the concrete, storing the concrete until you’re ready to mix it, or finding the necessary equipment for the job.

    Of course, there are a few drawbacks as well. For instance, because of the custom formulation from concrete experts, ready mix does tend to cost a little more up front compared to site mixed concrete. Also, this option is typically more cost-effective for larger batches of concrete.

    What is Site-Mixed Concrete?

    By contrast, you m ix site-mixed concrete at the project site, as the name suggests. Generally this involves picking up a bag of concrete mix from your local hardware store or home improvement store and mixing that with water. There are several differences between ready mix and site-mixed concrete.

    There are a few disadvantages to site-mixed concrete, the first being there’s less room for customization for the formula. In addition, because you’re mixing the concrete at the project site, there is often less accuracy in the water to concrete ratio. You also often need to have specialized equipment, such as a small cement mixer, as well as a qualified professional who knows what they are doing when mixing concrete for the best results. Typically, it’s slower to mix site-mixed concrete. This type of concrete is usually best for small batches of concrete where you don’t need precise formulation or mixing. Compared to Houston ready mix concrete, site-mixed concrete is often lower quality and can create quite a lot of waste. However, the benefits include potential cost-savings as well as not needing to schedule your concrete delivery.
