    Key Ingredients for Custom Concrete Mixes and Supply in Houston

    The backbone of concrete is cement as it serves as the fundamental binding agent in concrete. We provide different types of cement, each with unique properties suited for specific applications in construction. From Portland cement to specialty blends, choosing the right cement is crucial for achieving the desired strength, durability, and aesthetic qualities in a custom concrete mix.

    The basic components of Portland cement include limestone, clay, and gypsum. These raw materials are crushed and finely ground, and then heated in a kiln to produce clinker. The clinker is then ground into a fine powder, which is then considered Portland cement.

    What is the Strongest Concrete?

    The strength of concrete can vary depending on its composition and the specific mix design used. Generally, high-strength concrete refers to concrete with compressive strength significantly higher than that of regular concrete. The strength of concrete is usually measured in pounds per square inch (psi) or megapascals (MPa).

    Normal strength concrete is the most common type of concrete with a compressive strength typically ranging from 2,500 to 6,000 psi. It is used in a variety of general construction applications. High-strength concrete has higher compressive strength than normal-strength concrete. It is often used in high-rise buildings, bridges, and other structures where higher strength is required. Shotcrete, also known as sprayed concrete, is a type of concrete that is sprayed onto a surface using a high-velocity nozzle. It is often used for structural repair, tunnel construction, and slope stabilization. Pervious concrete has a high porosity, allowing water to pass through easily. It is used in applications where water runoff needs to be managed, such as in parking lots and pavements. With many concrete types to choose from, the Texan Concrete Supply team can suggest the right kind for your specific concrete construction needs.
