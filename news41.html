  • Houston Concrete Supply Tips for Stain Removal

    Posted on September 18, 2020 by Phoebee Johnson

    Can’t clean a stain off your concrete? Your Houston concrete supply offers resurfacing and replacement services to make sure your surfaces look brand new.

    After your Houston concrete supply installs your concrete surfaces, be sure to apply appropriate treatments or sealants regularly. These treatments help prevent concrete stains. However, if you do end up with stained concrete, there are a few things you can do. Depending on the type of stain, you may be able to clean it off your concrete surfaces. If not, consider resurfacing or replacement services from your Houston concrete supply.

    Cleaning Tips from Your Houston Concrete Supply

    Cleaning stained concrete can be a difficult task. However, your Houston concrete supply does have some tricks for common stains like rust, mold, algae, oil, and grease.

    Rust Stains

    Rust stains on concrete can occur when you leave metal objects on your concrete. For these types of stains, your Houston concrete supplier recommends using white vinegar on your concrete. First, pour the vinegar all over the stain, saturating it completely. Allow the vinegar to sit on your concrete for approximately half an hour. Then, use a plastic bristle brush to scrub the stain. Never use metal brushes on concrete because they can scar the surface. Once you’ve removed the stain, rinse with water.

    Mold and Algae Stains

    Mold and algae cause black, blue, or dark green stains on concrete. These stains can be quite difficult to remove, but your Houston concrete supply suggests using household bleach. The bleach helps kill the organisms and also can remove the stain from your concrete. Treat the stain by soaking it with bleach for about 15 minutes. For stubborn stains, you may need to scrub or power wash the area. Then, rinse the bleach away with water

    Oil and Grease Stains

    Oil and grease stains are notoriously hard to remove from concrete. This is because they seep into the porous surface of your concrete and often don’t respond to common cleaning solutions. As soon as you notice the grease or oil spot on your concrete, your Houston concrete supply recommends mopping it up with an absorbent cloth or paper towel as soon as possible. Then, try scrubbing the stain with dish soap or your preferred degreaser. If this doesn’t work, you can also try covering the stain in kitty litter and grinding it into the stain with your shoes. Don’t forget to sweep away the kitty litter and rinse the area when you’re done. However, if you can’t remove stains from your concrete, you may want to consider other options.

    Unable to Remove Stains? Talk to Your Houston Concrete Supply About Resurfacing or Replacing

    In some cases, you may not be able to clean stains out of concrete. This is especially true for old stains. However, replacing or resurfacing can restore the look of your concrete.
